Help! I keep hitting the snooze button and missing my early morning classes. Any tips to break the snooze addiction? As a dedicated snooze-smasher myself, I know that the struggle is real. Start with setting at least five alarms back to back before the actual wake-up time. The noise will wake someone up, and they can wake you up. Of course, how they wake you up, is not this author’s responsibility. In case you are one of those blessed souls who live entirely by themselves, invest in a rooster.If you get angry enough, you will have biriyani for dinner. Expectations vs. realities for first years. How do you cope? First years! Firstly, if you were expecting “Student of the Year” vibes, you are going to feel very “Three Idiots” at the end of your first two months. That said, be prepared to hear “Oo anta vaa mama” blaring through the campus grounds at least thrice a month. Secondly, contrary to popular belief, lunch breaks are not for relaxation. It’s Food Wars time, and tag team strategy is the way to go. Also, fledgling hostelites/ PG residents, please invest in stomach medicines. Lastly, I strongly recommend making your seniors your allies and friends. They have walked so you can run. And we all know running is a useful skill to get to your early morning classes. (P.S. Get the WiFi password from them.)
